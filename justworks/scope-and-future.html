Customer Central MVP · Prompt Requirements #8 & #9

What We're Not Building — and Why

Every item below was considered, scored, and deliberately deferred. The cuts are as important as the inclusions — they demonstrate that the MVP is shaped by discipline, not by what we ran out of time to include.

The Scoping Principle

An item is out of scope when the cost of including it — in complexity, timeline risk, or cognitive load — exceeds the cost of deferring it. But "deferred" doesn't mean "forgotten." Every item here has a revisit trigger: the specific condition that, when true, makes the item worth building next.

Section 1 Out of Scope for Phase 1 MVP
MVP + P2
Composite Customer Health Score
Lightweight rule-based score in MVP; ML-weighted adaptive scoring in Phase 2
▼
In MVP

Rule-based weighted score using billing, payroll, support, satisfaction, and activity signals. Formula: Base = (Billing x 0.30) + (Payroll x 0.25) + (Support x 0.20) + (Satisfaction x 0.15) + (Activity x 0.10), then Final = Base × Tenure Factor. Tenure Factor: <30 days = 0.75, 30-90 days = 0.85, 90-180 days = 0.95, 180+ days = 1.00. Health bands: 90-100 Green (Healthy), 70-89 Light Green (Good), 50-69 Amber (Attention), 30-49 Orange (At Risk), 0-29 Red (Critical). Transparent, decomposable, agent-verifiable. No ML in Phase 1 — simple rules agents can check against their own intuition. Leadership portfolio health list view for at-a-glance account assessment. Features: HS-1 (Lightweight Health Score), HS-2 (Leadership Health Overview). Week 2 manual validation with 5 experienced agents targets >=80% alignment.

Still Phase 2

ML-weighted adaptive scoring where signal weights adjust based on validated correlation data. Predictive churn indicators using historical pattern detection. Automated outreach triggers that fire proactive engagement based on health trajectory. Trend analysis over time showing health score movement over weeks/months. These require validated data patterns and agent trust in the simple version before upgrading to opaque models.

Why This Split

We CAN score with simple, transparent rules on Day 1. The data doesn't need 60 days of validation to be scored — it needs to exist and be queryable, which the MVP data foundation ensures. What requires validated data is ML-weighted scoring where agents can't verify the logic themselves. The rule-based score is the trust-building foundation that makes agents receptive to the ML upgrade in Phase 2. 11 total MVP features, 55 data fields.

Phase 2
In-Place Ticket Creation & Updates
Create and modify Zendesk tickets without leaving Customer Central
▼
Why It's Out

Read before write. The MVP proves that the consolidated read experience — seeing tickets, history, and context in one place — is accurate and trusted. Write-path errors are more dangerous than read-path errors. If an agent creates a ticket through Customer Central and it gets lost, duplicated, or malformed in Zendesk, that's a live customer impact. We need to prove the integration layer is solid before we push data back through it.

When to Revisit

When alpha and beta feedback confirm that agents trust the data they see and are requesting the ability to act on it without switching tabs. The signal is agents saying "I can see the problem, why can't I fix it from here?" — that pull-based demand validates the write path.

Risk If Built Too Early

Zendesk's ticket creation API has specific field validation, workflow triggers, and automation rules that interact with existing CSO processes. Building write-back without fully understanding these triggers could break existing automation (SLA tracking, routing rules, escalation triggers). Discovery with the Zendesk admin team is required first.

Phase 2
Benefits Life Event Workflows
Guided flows for marriage, baby, divorce, relocation — the multi-system cascades
▼
Why It's Out

Life events are the most complex call type CSO handles. A single event (e.g., employee has a baby) triggers changes across multiple carriers, state-specific rules, enrollment windows, tax withholdings, and COBRA implications. Building this correctly requires deep domain expertise and carrier-specific logic. Getting it wrong means employees get incorrect benefits coverage — that's a compliance and legal risk, not just a UX problem.

When to Revisit

When the benefits data surfacing in Phase 1 (PS-4: quick-view current elections + carrier deep link) has been validated as accurate, and when we've mapped the specific carrier APIs and state-rule engines needed to support guided workflows. Also requires partnership with the Benefits product team.

MVP Alternative

Phase 1 provides the agent with a quick-view of current benefits elections and a deep link to the carrier portal. This eliminates the "which plan is this employee on?" lookup but doesn't attempt to orchestrate the multi-system cascade. Agents continue to use existing tools for the actual changes — but with full context already loaded.

Phase 2
Compliance Contextual Guidance
State-specific rules and requirements surfaced based on the company's footprint
▼
Why It's Out

Surfacing compliance guidance requires a structured knowledge base that maps regulatory requirements to company attributes (state of operation, employee count, industry). This knowledge engine doesn't exist today — compliance information likely lives in internal wikis, training docs, and tribal knowledge. The data source is a knowledge base, not a database. Building the content taxonomy alone is a multi-week effort separate from the Customer Central engineering work.

When to Revisit

When Justworks has a structured compliance content system (either built internally or via a third-party provider) that can be queried programmatically by state + company attribute. OR when Phase 1 data reveals that compliance-related calls are a disproportionate driver of escalation and long handle times — at which point the business case justifies the content investment.

Strategic Value When Ready

Huge. Compliance is one of the primary reasons SMBs choose a PEO. If Customer Central can surface "this company operates in California, New York, and Texas — here are the requirements unique to each state" during a live call, it transforms the agent from someone who needs to look things up into a genuine compliance advisor. This is a moat-widening feature.

Phase 3
AI-Powered Interaction Summaries
NLP-generated summaries of past calls, chats, and emails
▼
Why It's Out

Two reasons. First, ML pipeline complexity: summarization requires model selection, fine-tuning on Justworks domain language, quality validation, and hallucination detection. That's a standalone initiative, not a feature bolted onto an MVP. Second, data quality: the AI needs structured interaction records to summarize well. Phase 1 builds those structured records (with tagged topics, resolution types, and outcome codes) — making Phase 3 summarization dramatically better than if we tried to summarize unstructured Zendesk comments today.

When to Revisit

When Phase 1's structured interaction records have accumulated 90+ days of data, providing enough volume and variety for quality evaluation. AND when the organization has made a decision about AI tooling (build vs. buy, model provider, data privacy guardrails for customer interaction data).

The Tiebreaker at Work

This is exactly why Phase 1 stores interactions as structured records with enums (topic tags, resolution types, sentiment codes) instead of free-text notes. Same MVP effort for agents — but structured data is training-ready data. When Phase 3 builds the summarization model, it won't need to parse millions of free-text fields first. The architectural choice in Phase 1 makes the AI feature in Phase 3 faster, cheaper, and more accurate.

Phase 3
Pre-emptive Problem Identification
Predict and flag issues before the customer notices them
▼
Why It's Out

This is the North Star — "the customer never needed to call in the first place" — but it requires pattern detection across historical data that Phase 1 is just starting to collect in structured form. To predict that a company will miss their payroll deadline, you need historical patterns of when companies miss deadlines. To detect declining engagement, you need a baseline of normal engagement. Phase 1 establishes those baselines.

When to Revisit

When there are two quarters of structured interaction + engagement data — enough to build correlation models between observable signals (login frequency decline, support contact spikes, payment failures) and outcomes (churn, escalation, downgrades). The predictive layer is only as good as the historical patterns it learns from.

Strategic Value When Ready

Transformative. This shifts CSO from reactive ("customer called with a problem") to proactive ("we flagged the risk and reached out before they called"). The prompt explicitly asks for this capability. It's the highest-value feature in the entire roadmap — which is precisely why it can't be rushed. A proactive alert system that fires false positives will train agents to ignore it, poisoning the well for the real thing.

Phase 3
Upsell & Cross-Sell Recommendations
Contextual product suggestions based on company profile and adoption gaps
▼
Why It's Out

Two concerns. Premature recommendations undermine trust. If the system suggests upgrading to PEO Plus while the agent is helping resolve a payroll error, the customer feels sold to, not served. The timing, context, and confidence of recommendations must be carefully calibrated. Second, conversion models don't exist yet. To recommend "Payroll customers with 15+ employees convert to PEO at X% rate," you need historical conversion data correlated with company attributes.

When to Revisit

When CSO leadership and Sales/RevOps have aligned on when and how agents should surface product suggestions during support interactions. This is a policy decision, not just a product decision. It also requires conversion analytics from the Sales team's Salesforce Opportunity data — which means partnership with Andres (RevOps).

Risk If Built Too Early

Agents who feel pressure to "sell" during support calls will resist the tool entirely. The prompt emphasizes that Justworks believes small businesses should "work directly with real people to solve their most complex problems." Upsell intelligence must be positioned as helping the customer get more value, not as a revenue extraction mechanism. That positioning requires organizational alignment that may not exist yet.

Phase 2
Telephony / CTI Screen-Pop Integration
Caller ID auto-matches to customer profile when the phone rings
▼
Why It's Out

Third-party dependency on the critical path. We don't know which telephony system CSO uses (Five9, Talkdesk, Genesys, RingCentral — each has different CTI APIs). Adding a telephony integration to the MVP introduces a vendor dependency, a separate integration timeline, and a separate testing surface. The MVP value — consolidated context once you know who's calling — doesn't depend on CTI. Agents can still search by name or phone number in <2 seconds.

When to Revisit

When the MVP is live and we've confirmed: (a) which telephony system is in use, (b) whether their CTI API supports the integration pattern we need, and (c) whether the 2-second search is actually a meaningful bottleneck vs. the 30-60 seconds it takes today. If the search works well enough, CTI may be lower priority than other Phase 2 items.

MVP Alternative

⌘K search with results in <2 seconds. Agents type the first few characters of a name, company, or phone number and the match appears. This covers 95% of the CTI value — the remaining 5% is eliminating the typing step entirely. Worth pursuing, but not worth blocking the MVP.

Phase 2
Knowledge Base / Suggested Articles
Contextual help content surfaced based on the caller's issue
▼
Why It's Out

Similar to compliance guidance: requires a structured, searchable knowledge base with topic taxonomy. If the internal KB is a collection of Google Docs and Confluence pages (common for companies at this stage), there's no API to query contextually. Building the content infrastructure is a prerequisite — and that's a separate initiative owned by CSO Ops or Training, not the product team building Customer Central.

When to Revisit

When the internal knowledge base has been migrated to a structured, API-accessible system (e.g., Guru, Notion API, or a custom solution). OR when the AI summarization capability (Phase 3) can dynamically generate guidance from interaction history — essentially learning the KB from how experienced agents resolve issues.

MVP + P2
Leadership Reporting & Analytics Views
Lightweight portfolio health list in MVP; full BI-grade reporting and trend analytics in Phase 2
▼
In MVP

Lightweight portfolio health list view (HS-2) for team leads and CSO leadership. Shows all accounts with health scores, filterable by health band (Green/Light Green/Amber/Orange/Red). Simple list — not a full BI dashboard. This is the natural complement to the agent-facing health score: agents see individual account health, leadership sees the portfolio. Same data, different lens.

Still Phase 2

Full BI-grade reporting and trend analytics — aggregate dashboards with drill-down, health trend charts over time, team performance views, cohort analysis, and VP-level executive summaries. These require accumulated data (weeks/months of health scores) and more complex visualization. Natalie Miranda's team can build Tableau dashboards against the same data using existing reporting infrastructure in the interim.

Why This Split

A portfolio list is a lightweight extension of the health score — it's the same data rendered as a list instead of an individual badge. Adding it to MVP is ~3-4 days of frontend engineering, not a doubling of the design surface. Full BI reporting is a separate initiative with different user needs, permission models, and visualization requirements. The API-first architecture means we're not blocking full reporting — we're just scoping it appropriately.

Later
Multi-Language Support
Dashboard and content localization for non-English interactions
▼
Why It's Out

No signal that CSO currently operates in multiple languages. Justworks is a U.S.-focused PEO serving primarily English-speaking SMBs. The EOR and International Contractors products are newer and lower-volume. Internationalization adds localization complexity to every string, label, and data format in the UI — effort that has zero impact on the current user base.

When to Revisit

When the EOR/International Contractors customer base grows to a point where non-English support interactions are a measurable percentage of volume. Or when Justworks hires non-English-speaking agents for dedicated international support pods.

Later
Customer Self-Service Portal Linking
Bi-directional sync between agent view and customer-facing portal
▼
Why It's Out

Different user, different team, different product. The self-service portal is a customer-facing product owned by a different product team. Linking them requires API contracts, data synchronization policies, and cross-team coordination that would pull focus from the core CSO agent experience. Customer Central is an internal tool — the self-service portal is an external one. They'll eventually share data infrastructure (the API-first architecture makes this possible) but they shouldn't share a delivery timeline.

When to Revisit

When the self-service portal team is ready to consume the Customer Central API for shared data (e.g., showing customers their own interaction history or ticket status). The architecture supports it — the organizational alignment needs to happen first.

Everything above is a deliberate deferral, not an oversight. The Phase 1 MVP is scoped to prove a focused thesis: agents with unified context resolve issues faster and more accurately. Once that's proven, the deferred items become the Phase 2 and 3 roadmap — each building on the data foundation and trust the MVP establishes.

Section 2 Feature Requests Anticipated in Future Versions
Phase 2
Advanced Health Scoring — ML-Weighted & Predictive
Upgrading the MVP's transparent score to an adaptive, predictive intelligence layer
▼
Phase 2 is the upgrade, not the introduction. The MVP already delivers a rule-based composite health score with transparent weights. Phase 2 evolves this into ML-weighted adaptive scoring where signal weights adjust based on validated correlation data — because different signals matter differently for Payroll-only vs. PEO Basic vs. PEO Plus customers. A Payroll customer who logs in once a month to run payroll is healthy; a PEO Plus customer with the same login pattern might be underutilizing their plan. Adds predictive churn indicators, trend analytics over time, and automated outreach triggers.
Business Value

Enables proactive retention at scale. The MVP score identifies current state; the ML upgrade predicts trajectory. CSO leadership can identify accounts trending toward risk before they arrive, prioritize outbound outreach by predicted trajectory, and measure whether interventions change outcomes. Directly impacts net revenue retention.

Technical Approach

ML pipeline consuming the aggregation layer's structured data plus the MVP health score history. Regression analysis correlating signal patterns with actual churn outcomes. Configurable weight UI for CSO leadership to adjust by segment. The MVP's rule-based score provides the labeled baseline — "this account scored 45 and churned" becomes training data for the ML model.

MVP Foundation Required

The MVP health score (HS-1) must be live and validated — agents confirming the simple score matches their intuition (>=80% alignment target). Health score history must be accumulating (60+ days of scored data). The leadership portfolio view (HS-2) must be in use so leadership has context for the upgrade. Phase 1 builds the score; Phase 2 makes it intelligent.

Phase 2
Proactive Alert System
See problems forming and reach out before the customer calls
▼
Automated flags for at-risk accounts pushed to a CSO queue for outbound outreach. The MVP health score provides the substrate — the rule-based composite score already identifies accounts in Amber/Orange/Red bands. The alert system adds automation and outreach triggers on top: 3+ missed payments, declining login frequency over 4 weeks, cancellation page visits, support volume spike (3x normal rate), NPS detractor response, payroll deadline approaching for a company that missed last quarter's deadline. Each alert includes recommended action and context packet.
Business Value

This is the North Star in action. Shifts CSO from reactive to proactive. A company that missed their payroll deadline last quarter gets a call the week before this quarter's — that's the kind of service that earns NPS of +60. Reduces churn by catching warning signs early. Creates "wow moments" that reinforce the Justworks brand promise.

Technical Approach

Rule-based engine initially (threshold triggers), evolving to ML-based (pattern detection). Start simple: "if missed_payments > 2 AND login_30d < 3, flag as at-risk." Validate against outcomes. Graduate to predictive models as data accumulates. Critical: must include false-positive management — alert fatigue kills proactive systems faster than anything.

MVP Foundation Required

Requires reliable historical baselines (what does "normal" look like for each account type), which means the Phase 1 aggregation layer must be running for at least one quarter. Also requires CSO workflow changes — proactive outbound is a different motion than reactive inbound. Operational readiness is as important as technical readiness.

Phase 3
Upsell & Cross-Sell Intelligence
Every support interaction is a potential value expansion moment
▼
Contextual product suggestions surfaced to agents based on company profile, growth trajectory, and adoption gaps. Examples: Payroll-only customer with 15+ employees → PEO recommendation with estimated savings. PEO Basic customer adding employees in new states → PEO Plus for automatic multi-state compliance. Company not using time tracking → time tracking add-on suggestion after the 3rd manual payroll adjustment.
Business Value

CSO-influenced revenue. Every expansion conversation that originates from a support interaction is attributable revenue. This transforms CSO from a cost center into a revenue contributor — which changes how the organization thinks about support investment. Requires careful positioning: agents are advisors helping customers get more value, not salespeople with quotas.

Technical Approach

Conversion models built on historical upgrade data from Salesforce Opportunities. Identify which company attributes and behaviors correlate with successful plan upgrades. Start with heuristic rules ("Payroll customer, 15+ employees, 6+ months tenure → show PEO prompt"), evolve to propensity scoring. Critical: timing and context sensitivity — never show upsell during an active issue resolution.

Dependencies

Requires alignment between CSO, Sales, and RevOps on when and how agents surface product suggestions. Andres (RevOps) needs to define attribution rules. Sales leadership needs to agree on CSO's role in the expansion motion. This is 50% organizational alignment, 50% product build.

Risk

If agents feel like they're being asked to sell, they'll resist the entire tool. The prompt explicitly says Justworks believes small businesses should "work directly with real people to solve their most complex problems." Upsell must be framed as problem-solving ("you're managing compliance manually across 3 states — PEO Plus automates that"), not as sales. Get this wrong and you poison agent trust.

Phase 3
AI-Powered Interaction Intelligence
Turn every conversation into organizational learning
▼
NLP analysis of call transcripts, chat logs, and email threads to identify trending issues, sentiment patterns, resolution path effectiveness, and training opportunities. Not just summarizing individual interactions — analyzing the aggregate to surface insights no single agent could see. "Call volume about benefits enrollment confusion spiked 40% this week" or "agents who reference the payroll calculator resolve pay discrepancy calls 30% faster."
Business Value

Closes the feedback loop. The prompt says customer interactions are "an informative proprietary data set to learn from and action off of." This feature operationalizes that vision. Product teams learn what customers struggle with. Training teams learn which resolution approaches work best. Leadership gets real-time pulse on customer sentiment. The interaction data becomes a strategic asset, not just a support log.

Technical Approach

NLP pipeline consuming the structured interaction records from Phase 1. Topic modeling to cluster common issues. Sentiment analysis to track satisfaction trends. Resolution path analysis to identify best practices. Anomaly detection to flag emerging issues (e.g., new product release causing a call spike). Outputs feed into both agent-facing dashboards and leadership reporting.

MVP Foundation Required

This is where the Tiebreaker Principle pays off most dramatically. Phase 1's decision to store interactions with structured topic tags, resolution types, and outcome codes means the Phase 3 NLP pipeline doesn't start from raw text — it starts from semi-structured data with human-generated labels. The tagging system in Phase 1 essentially creates a labeled training dataset for Phase 3's models.

Phase 3+
Agent Copilot
Real-time AI assistance during live interactions
▼
AI that listens to the conversation in real-time and provides suggested responses, relevant knowledge articles, similar past resolutions, and next-best-action prompts. Not replacing the agent — augmenting them. Think of it as giving every agent the pattern recognition of the most experienced person on the team. "The customer is describing a situation similar to 47 previous calls — here's how the best agents resolved those."
Business Value

Scalable expertise. This is how Justworks scales from 10K to 50K customers without 5x-ing CSO headcount. New agents ramp faster because they have AI-assisted guidance. Experienced agents handle edge cases faster because similar resolutions are surfaced instantly. The quality bar stays high even as the team grows — because the institutional knowledge is encoded in the system, not trapped in individuals' heads.

Technical Approach

Requires: real-time speech-to-text (or chat stream processing), intent classification, context-aware retrieval from the interaction history and knowledge base, and a suggestion UI that's helpful without being distracting. Technically the most complex feature in the entire roadmap. Build only when the data foundation, interaction intelligence, and knowledge base are all mature.

The Long-Term Vision

This is the feature that turns Customer Central from a dashboard into a copilot. The MVP is the agent's workspace. Phase 2 adds intelligence. Phase 3 adds prediction. The Copilot is the culmination — where the system doesn't just show the agent information, it actively partners with them to deliver the best possible service. The prompt's aspiration — "pre-empt problems before they arise and proactively provide support" — lives here.

The pattern across every scope decision: Phase 1 builds the data infrastructure AND a lightweight, transparent health score agents can verify. Phase 2 upgrades that score to ML-weighted adaptive intelligence and adds full reporting. Phase 3 acts on that intelligence autonomously with predictions and proactive outreach. Each phase earns the right to the next by proving accuracy, earning trust, and accumulating the structured data that makes the next capability possible. Nothing is deferred arbitrarily — and what's included (like the rule-based health score) is included because transparent rules build the trust foundation that makes opaque ML models acceptable later.
